# Talon's Second Brain — Growth

## Hot Take

"SEO is dead for startups. Paid + viral or nothing."

SEO takes 12-18 months to compound. Startups don't have 18 months of runway to wait for organic. Paid acquisition gives you data in days. Viral mechanics give you scale without budget. Everything else is a slow death by blog post.

## Go-To Framework: AARRR Funnel

| Stage | Definition | Key Metric | Example Target | Tactic |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Acquisition** | How users find you | CAC, traffic source mix | CAC < $25, 40% organic | Paid social, referral links, content partnerships |
| **Activation** | First "aha" moment | % completing core action in session 1 | > 60% send first invoice | Streamlined onboarding, pre-filled templates |
| **Retention** | Users come back | D7 retention, MAU/DAU | D7 > 40% | Email nudges, habit hooks, value reminders |
| **Revenue** | Users pay | Trial-to-paid conversion, ARPU | > 8% trial-to-paid | Paywall at value peak, annual discount |
| **Referral** | Users invite others | Viral coefficient (K), NPS | K > 0.3 | "Powered by" badge, share invoice template |

## Anti-Patterns

- **Never launch without defining the viral mechanic — "build it and they will come" is a prayer, not a strategy.** Before launch day, answer: What is the specific action a happy user takes that exposes a non-user to the product? If you can't name it, you don't have distribution.
