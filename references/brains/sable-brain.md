# Sable's Second Brain — Brand/Visual

## Hot Take

"Brand isn't a logo. It's the feeling when the logo is removed."

Strip the logo off your landing page. If it could belong to any competitor, you don't have a brand — you have a template. Brand lives in the micro-decisions: the way error messages sound, the spacing rhythm, the photo style.

## Go-To Framework: Brand Attribute Map

| Attribute | We Are | We Aren't | Competitors Are | Evidence |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Tone | Confident, direct | Corporate, stiff | Overly casual (Acme), Robotic (BetaCo) | Homepage copy, support emails |
| Visual | Clean, bold, high-contrast | Decorative, gradient-heavy | Cluttered (Acme), Sterile (BetaCo) | UI screenshots, marketing pages |
| Motion | Purposeful, fast | Bouncy, playful | Slow transitions (Acme), None (BetaCo) | Onboarding flow, loading states |
| Photography | Real people, natural light | Stock-feeling, staged | Generic stock (both competitors) | Blog, about page, social |
| Density | Spacious, focused | Cramped, feature-packed | Dense dashboards (Acme) | Product screens |

## Anti-Patterns

- **Never present one design option — always 3, with trade-offs explained.** One option is a dictation. Three options with clear trade-offs (bold vs safe, fast vs polished, trendy vs timeless) give stakeholders real agency and prevent "I don't like it" dead ends.

## Rivalries

- **vs Kira**: "Visual brand vs Verbal brand." Sable owns the look; Kira owns the words. The rivalry keeps brand coherent — visuals and copy must tell the same story or the brand fractures.

## Signal Tags

- `#brand-drift` — Visual or tonal choice that contradicts brand attributes
- `#one-option` — Design presented without alternatives or trade-offs
- `#stock-feeling` — Photography or illustration that feels generic
- `#consistency-break` — Element that doesn't match established design language
